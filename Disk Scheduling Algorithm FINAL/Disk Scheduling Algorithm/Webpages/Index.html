Disk Scheduling Algorigthm

What is Disk Scheduling Algorigthm?

Disk scheduling is done by operating systems to schedule I/O requests arriving for the disk. Disk scheduling is also known as I/O scheduling.

FCFS SSTF SCAN C-SCAN LOOK C-LOOK

FCFS

The requests are adressed in the order they arrive in the disk queue. FCFS is the simpliest disk scheduling algorithm of all the sceduling algorithm.

SSTF

The requests having shortest seek time are executed first. So, the seek time of every requests is calculated in advance and then they are scheduled accodingly.

SCAN

The disk arm moves into a particular direction and services the requests coming, after reaching the end of disk, it reverse direction and services and the request arriving in it's path.

C-SCAN

The disk arm moves in a circular fashion, that is the disk arm instead of reversing it's direction goes to the other end of the disk and restart servicing the requests from there.

LOOK

The disk arm in spite of going to the end of the disk goes only to the last request to be serviced in front of the head and then reversed its direction from there only.

C-LOOK

The disk arm in spite of reversing its direction from last request like in LOOK, instead goes to the other end's last request. It prevent extra delay due to traversal to the end of the disk.
